24. Contoh analytical exposition tentang having exercise is good for our body

Beside physical advantage, exercise also brings good effects mentally. Due to the fitter body, exercise can make us feel refreshed and happy then we can increase our life quality and expectancy.

30. exercise 1 and exercise 2​


Jawaban:

Lihat lampiran ya..

Penjelasan:

Pronoun/ kata ganti, adalah kata yang merupakan sebutan/panggilan untuk  menggantikan orang atau benda.

Contohnya:

Kemarin ayah tidak pergi ke kantor. Dia mengunjungi nenek.

(Yesterday my father didn't go to work. He visited my grandmother)

Kata Dia/He di kalimat di atas adalah pronoun/kata ganti, yaitu untuk menggantikan my father/ayah saya.

Kata ganti bisa dikelompokkan apakah dia sebagai subjek, objek atau pemilik sesuatu di dalam sebuah kalimat.

Sebagai subjek pronoun adalah:

I, you, he, she, it, we, you (jamak) dan they.

Sebagai objek, pronoun adalah:

Me (dari subjek I)

You (dari subjek you)

Him (dari subjek (He)

Her (dari subjek She)

It (dari subjek It)

Us (dari subjek (We)

Them (dari subjek they)

Contoh:

pronoun they (mereka)

They call me

(they adalah subject pronoun, karena dia berfungsi sebagai subjek kalimat)

I call them

(they berubah jadi them, karena posisinya bukan subjek tapi object kalimat)

Jadi them di sini adalah object pronoun.

Contoh lain: Saya /I

I call them (I sebagai subjek kalimat)

They call me (I berubah menjaid me, karena posisinya adalah objek kalimat)

Selain subjek dan objek kalimat, pronoun juga ada yang menggambarkan kepemilikan.

Misalnya: bolaku, ayahmu, rumah mereka dst

Ini disebut possessive adjective, dipakai dengan harus mencantumkan benda/noun yang dimiliki tersebut.

bolaku (my ball), ayahmu (your father), rumah mereka (their house)

Yang termasuk possessive adjective adalah:

I  ===> my

you ===> your

he ===> his

She ===> her

it ===> its

we ===> our

they ===> their

Selain itu ada possessive pronoun yaitu kata ganti milik yang tidak perlu menyebutkan barang/bendanya.

dalam bahasa indonesia biasanya dipakai kata "punya" ku/mu/dia dst,

Yang termasuk possessive pronoun:

mine (punyaku)

yours (punyamu)

his (punyanya dia laki-laki)

hers (punyanya dia perempuan)

its (punyanya dia/benda/hewan)

ours (punyanya kami/kita)

theirs (punya mereka)

Contoh:

The ball you are holding is mine (bola yang sedang kamu pegang itu adalah punyaku)

I just borrowed this book. It's hers (aku cuma minjam buku ini. Ini miliknya dia (perempuan)

37. exercise kerjakan soal nya ada di foto di atas !!!​


Jawaban:

1. (+) I have been waiting for you here for six hours

(-) I haven't been waiting for you here for six hours

(?) Have I been waiting for you here for six hours?

2. (+) Their father have been singing since yesterday

(-) Their father haven't been singing since yesterday

(?) Have their father been singing since yesterday?

3. (+) She has been doing the English task for 55 minutes

(-) She hasn't been doing the English .............

(?) Has she been doing the English .........?

4. (+) They have been playing football for .......

(-) They haven't been playing football for ......

(?) Have they been playing football for 3 hours?

5. (+) Their grandma has been watering the ........

(-) Their grandma hasn't been watering the flowers since this morning

(?) Has their grandma been watering the .......?

6. (+) The cat became viral yesterday

(-) The cat did not become viral yesyerday

(?) Did the cat become viral yesterday?

7. (+) The singer sang a beautiful song last night

(-) The singer did not sing a beautiful song last night

(?) Did the singer sing a ........ ?

8. (+) We visited the old temple 2 months ago

(-) We did not visit the old .........

(?) Did we visit the old ........ ?

9. (+) The English teacher made some tasks yesterday

(-) The English teacher did not make some ........

(?) Did the English teacher make some ......... ?

10. (+) I gave the homeless the food yesterday

(-) I did not give the homeless the ......

(?) Did I give the homeless the ......... ?

Penjelasan:

Present perfect continuous

(+) S + Have/Has been + Ving

(-) S + Have not/has not been + Ving

(?) Have/has + S + been + Ving ?

Past Tense

(+) S + V2 + O

(-) S + did not + V1 + O

(?) Did + S + V1 + O ?
