Jawaban:

The generic participant of the text is water.

The goal of the text is to explain the cycle of water, specifically focusing on how rain is formed and how water moves through various stages such as clouds, rain, rivers, and oceans.

Sequenced explanation of the text:

Rain always comes from clouds.

Clouds are formed when water evaporates from warm bodies of water, such as oceans, due to the heat of the sun.

The evaporated water forms clouds in the sky.

The wind carries the clouds for long distances.

When the clouds meet cold air in the sky, the water vapor condenses and forms drops of water.

These drops of water fall from the clouds as rain.

The rainwater runs into rivers.

Rivers flow into oceans.

The water in the oceans then evaporates again and the cycle repeats.

The text emphasizes that water is constantly moving through this cycle, from oceans to clouds to rain to rivers to oceans again.

The concluding statement highlights that the water we experience in our daily lives has been part of this cycle and has been on other heads and in other gardens before.

2. The goal of the text is to explain where rain comes from and how the water cycle works.

- Clouds are formed when water evaporates from the oceans due to the sun's heat.

- The evaporated water forms clouds in the sky.

- Therefore, water is always moving through the cycle from oceans to clouds to rain to rivers and back to the oceans.

The statement “two ears in the head of ondel ondel” is a description of the traditional Indonesian folk puppet known as ondel-ondel. Ondel-ondel is a large puppet figure, often made of bamboo, cloth, and painted in bright colors. It is commonly associated with Betawi culture in Jakarta, Indonesia.

Ondel-ondel puppets typically have exaggerated features, including a large head with two ears. These ears are attached to the sides of the head and are designed to enhance the visual appearance of the puppet. They are usually made of cloth or other lightweight materials.

The purpose of the ears in the ondel-ondel puppet is primarily decorative, contributing to the overall aesthetic appeal and playfulness of the figure.

The Rain

Rain always comes from clouds. But where do clouds come from? How does all the water get into the sky?

Think about your bathroom. There is hot water in your bath. Steam goes up from the hot water. The steam makes small clouds in the bathroom. These warm clouds meet the cold walls and windows. Then we see small drops of water on the walls and windows.

The world is like your bathroom. The water in the oceans is warm when the sun shines on it. Some of this water goes up into the sky and makes clouds. The wind carries the clouds for hundreds of kilometers. The clouds meet the cold air in the sky. Then we’ll see drops of water after the condensation of clouds to water. The drops of water are rain.

The rain falls and runs into rivers. Rivers run into oceans. And the water from the oceans changes into clouds and becomes rain. So water is always moving from oceans to clouds to rain to rivers to oceans.
