35. Change these sentences into simple present tense or present continuous tense. positive, negative and interrogative sentences! 1. She play football with her friends in the field now. 2. I and my friend study English every day.3. My mother (be) my sunshine in my live.​


1. Simple Present Tense:

- Positive: She plays football with her friends in the field now.

- Negative: She does not play football with her friends in the field now.

- Interrogative: Does she play football with her friends in the field now?

Present Continuous Tense:

- Positive: She is playing football with her friends in the field now.

- Negative: She is not playing football with her friends in the field now.

- Interrogative: Is she playing football with her friends in the field now?

2. Simple Present Tense:

- Positive: I and my friend study English every day.

- Negative: I and my friend do not study English every day.

- Interrogative: Do I and my friend study English every day?

Present Continuous Tense:

- Positive: I and my friend are studying English right now.

- Negative: I and my friend are not studying English right now.

- Interrogative: Are I and my friend studying English right now?

3. Simple Present Tense:

- Positive: My mother is my sunshine in my life.

- Negative: My mother is not my sunshine in my life.

- Interrogative: Is my mother my sunshine in my life?

Present Continuous Tense:

- Positive: My mother is being my sunshine in my life.

- Negative: My mother is not being my sunshine in my life.

- Interrogative: Is my mother being my sunshine in my life?
