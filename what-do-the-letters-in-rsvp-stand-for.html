Penjelasan:

The "C" in International Spelling Board (ISB) stands for "Congress." The ISB is a body of experts and scholars that is responsible for overseeing the development and use of the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA). The IPA is a standardized system of phonetic notation that is used to represent the sounds of spoken language in a consistent and precise manner.

The ISB was established in 1886 at the first International Phonetic Congress, which was held in Paris. The Congress was attended by a group of leading linguists, phoneticians, and scholars from various countries who were interested in the study of phonetics and the development of a standardized system for representing the sounds of language. At the Congress, the attendees agreed to form the ISB and to begin work on a new alphabet that could be used to accurately transcribe the sounds of spoken language.

Since its founding, the ISB has played a key role in the development and dissemination of the IPA. The organization holds regular meetings and conferences, publishes research and guides on the use of the IPA, and provides advice and support to linguists and scholars who use the alphabet in their work. The ISB also maintains the official website of the IPA, where users can access resources and information about the alphabet and its use.

In summary, the "C" in International Spelling Board stands for "Congress," as the organization was founded at the first International Phonetic Congress in Paris.
