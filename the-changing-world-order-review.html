|| PRESNET CONTINUOUS TENSE ||

Kalimat positive (+)

→ S + to be (is/am/are) + verb-ing + O

Contoh :

We are studying English now.My mother is cooking in the kitchen at the moment.They are playing basketball.She is doing her homework right now.

Kalimat negative (-)

→ S + to be (is/am/are) + not + verb-ing + O

Contoh :

We aren't studying English now.My mother isn't cooking in the kitchen at the moment.They aren't playing basketball.She isn't doing her homework right now.

Kalimat nterrogative (?)

→ to be (is/am/are) + S + verb-ing + O

Contoh :

Are we studying English now?Is my mother cooking in the kitchen at the moment?Are they playing basketball?Is she doing her homework right now?

Note :

Subject → to be

I → amHe, she, it → isThey, we, you → are

12. The school had the students ... their lessons before the exam. A. To review B. Reviewing C. Review D. Reviewed E. They review


CAUSATIVE MOOD

The school had the students review their lessons before the exam.

-------------

EXPLANATION

Causative mood adalah salah satu modus dalam bahasa Inggris.

Pada kalimat yang menggunakan causative mood, subjek kalimat bukanlah pelaku aksi melainkan yang menyebabkan orang lain melakukan aksi yang dimaksud.

Kata kerja yang biasa digunakan dalam causative mood antara lain:

letmakehaveget

Kalimat active causative mempunyai konstruksi:

S + let/make/have + person + infinitive (v₁)

atau

S + get + person + to infinitive

Contoh:

I let my mother cut my hair.

I had the barber cut my hair.

I got the barber to cut my hair.

--------------------

EXPLICATION

Soal merupakan kalimat dalam active causative mood dan causative verb yang digunakan adalah have, maka kata kerja non-finite yang kita gunakan dalam bentuk infinitive (v₁).

Jadi, opsi yang tepat adalah C. review.

17. The school master had the students .. their lessons before the exam. a. to review b. reviewing c. review d. reviewed


The school master had the students reviewed their lessons before the exam. (D)

Tense yang digunakan pada kalimat ini past perfect (had + past participle)

Pembahasan

Untuk menjawab soal ini, perhatikan kata 'had'. Kata 'had' diikuti dengan past participle atau verb 3.

Past participle dari review adalah reviewed.

A. to review digunakan pada satu kalimat yang sebelumnya telah memilih kata kerja. "To review" termasuk ke dalam to infinitive.

B. Reviewing digunakan pada kalimat progressive atau continuous. Kata 'reviewing' sendiri termasuk present participle yang sebelumnya terdapat auxiliary verb (am/is/are/was/were)

C. Review digunakan pada simple present tense dengan subject jamak.

Perhatikan kalimat berikut ini.

The school master had to review the students' lessons before the exam.

Pada kalimat ini, had to berarti harus memeriksa.

40. Cariin review the jungle book


Visually stunning and expertly acted, this retelling of a classic pays tribute to the original adventure while erasing the insensitivity of parts of Disney's '60s version. Sethi is a compelling young lead, making viewers really care about a boy who has only known the jungle and doesn't understand why he's being hunted. Elba is pitch perfect as the villainous Shere Khan, who in a misguided way makes sense -- man doescause destruction in the jungle -- but is so blinded by vengeance that he can't be made to see that Mowgli truly loves the jungle. All of the supporting actors are strong as their animal counterparts, and (with the exception of Murray and Christopher Walken, whose voices are too iconic to forget who they are) they don't come off as mere A-list cameos. With her husky purr, Scarlett Johansson is very well cast as giant python Kaa, who hypnotizes Mowgli with the tale of the boy's own origins.

There are only a few musical numbers in The Jungle Book, all of which are rearranged from the original: Kaa's transfixing "Trust in Me" (which really doesn't show up until the credits), Baloo's "Bare Necessities," and "I Wanna Be Like You," which Walken sings as the ambitious Gigantopithecus (an extinct giant orangutan) King Louie. That character's portrayal was problematic in the original Disney film, prompting criticism for being racist, and it's wonderful that director Jon Favreau's interpretation of the story isn't culturally insensitive (just scary -- Louie is huge!). The pacing can be leisurely, but there are also plenty of heart-stopping thrills and action sequences to keep audiences riveted -- and, in a few cases, jumping out of their seats.
