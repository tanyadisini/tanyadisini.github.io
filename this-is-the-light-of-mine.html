13. This is an input device that works by shining a light onto the surface of a document. The light source is automatically moved across the document and the reflected light is captured by mirrors and lenses.​


Jawaban:

The device you are referring to is called a scanner. A scanner is an input device that uses light to capture images or documents and convert them into a digital format. It uses a light source, typically a light-emitting diode (LED) or a fluorescent lamp, to illuminate the document. The light that reflects off the document is then directed through a series of mirrors and lenses to focus the image onto a photosensitive sensor, such as a Charge-Coupled Device (CCD) or a Contact Image Sensor (CIS). These sensors convert the captured light into digital signals that can be processed and stored on a computer or other electronic device. Scanners are commonly used for digitizing documents, photos, and other printed materials.

36. the amount of light entering the eye is controlled by the​


Jawaban:

The amount of light entering the eye is controlled by the​ iris.

Penjelasan:

The iris can adjust the size of the pupil in response to change in the amount of light in the environment. When there is bright light, the iris constricts the pupil to reduce the amount of light entering the eye. When there is dim light, the iris dilates the pupil to allow more light to enter the eye. This process is called pupil reflex and it helps to maintain optimal visual acuity in different lighting conditions.
