2. What are the characteristics of derivational affixes? ​


Penjelasan:

Derivational affixes are subdivided into two groups: class-changing and class-maintaining. Class-changing derivational affixes change the word class to which they are added. Thus, the verb achieve and the suffix –able create an adjective achievable.

Jawaban:

A derivational affix is an affix by means of which one word is formed (derived) from another.

5. contoh affixes,praffixes,saffixes itu apa sebutkan minimal 9 contoh ya ..... :) dan terdapat synomim dan antonymnya ya


Answer:

Affix is several joint letters called morphemes (like -ed, -es, -ness, un-, pre-) which are attached either before a word or after a word. Affix before a word is called prefix, while the one after the word is called suffix. Affixes can also be derivational like -ity, -ness or inflectional -ed, -s. Here are the nine example of them.
Affix 
- faded / antennas / benefactor / mouthful / enlighten / enrichment / impossible / likely / likelihood.
Suffix (after the words)
sadness / happiness / stupidity / creativity / acting / lying / careful / careless / normalization
Prefix (before the words)
undo/ undress / disengage / disarm / dispossess / preteach / endanger / encourage
For the synonym and antonym, you can see it from the suffix parts. 

Kelas: SMA
Pelajaran: English
Kata kunci: preffix, suffix, word

15. Why is [-ment] in 'agreement' called a derivational bound morpheme?.


Because Derivational morphemes are bound morphemes or affixes which derive or create new words by either changing the meaning or the part of speech or both.

In this case, the base word is "agree" which means to accept or to go along with something while adding "-ment" to the end of the word "agree" creating a new meaning where "Agreement" is the act of coming to a mutual decision, position or arrangement.
