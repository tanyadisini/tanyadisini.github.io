30. What do you know about present future? Meaning, pattern and 3 examples!​


Jawaban:

I assume you are referring to the future tense in English. The future tense is used to talk about actions or events that will happen at some point in the future. Here's some information about it, along with examples:

**Meaning:** The future tense is used to express actions or situations that will occur after the present moment or at some point in the future.

**Pattern:** In English, there are several ways to form the future tense. The most common ones include:

1. **Will + Base Verb:** This is the most common way to form the future tense.

- Example: "I will travel to Paris next week."

2. **Be Going To + Base Verb:** This is used when you have a plan or intention to do something in the future.

- Example: "She is going to start a new job in September."

3. **Present Continuous Tense:** Sometimes, the present continuous tense is used to talk about future plans or arrangements.

- Example: "They are meeting at the cafe tomorrow."

**Three Examples:**

1. I **will visit** my grandmother on Sunday. (Using "will" to express a future action)

2. They **are going to watch** a movie tonight. (Using "be going to" for future plans)

3. He **is flying** to London tomorrow morning. (Using present continuous for future arrangements)

These examples illustrate different ways to express future actions or events in English. Remember that the choice of tense depends on the context and the speaker's intention.

Jawaban:

Past perfect tense nomina :

Positive : subjek + had + been + pelengkap + ...

Negative : subjek + had not + been + pelengkap + ...

Interrogative : had + subjek + been + pelengkap + ...

Past Perfect Tense verba :

Positive : Subjek + had + verb 3 + objek + ket.waktu

Negative : subjek + had not + been + objek + Ket.waktu

Interrogative : had + subjek + verb 3 + objek + ket.waktu
