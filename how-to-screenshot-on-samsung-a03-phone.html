4. We....(to talk) on the phone for thrty minutes


JAWABAN:

PRESENT PERFECT TENSE

We....(to talk) on the phone for thirty minutes

Di dalam kalimat di atas terdapat keterangan "for thirty minutes" yang mengindikasikan bahwa kalimat tersebut dalam bentuk present perfect tense.

Sesuai dengan rumus present perfect tense "Subject + have/has + Verb 3", maka jawabannya adalah:

=> We have talked on the phone for thirty minutes. (Kami sudah berbicara di telepon selama tiga puluh menit)

PEMBAHASAN:

Present Perfect Tense

Present perfect tense merupakan suatu tense yang menggambarkan suatu kejadian yang terjadi sebelum satu spesifik waktu di masa lalu tetapi masih berlanjut sampai sekarang. Tense ini terdiri dari gabungan aspek perfect (sebelum satu spesifik waktu di masa lampau) dan bingkai waktu present (masa sekarang).

Selain itu, tense ini juga bisa menggambarkan suatu kejadian yang dimulai dan berakhir di masa lampau tetapi efeknya masih dirasakan sampai saat sekarang. Lebih singkatnya, tense ini digunakan sebagai penghubung antara masa lalu dan masa sekarang.

Rumus Present Perfect Tense

Present perfect tense dibentuk dengan  auxiliary verb have atau has  dan past participle (verb 3). Have berpasangan dengan subjek  I, You, We, dan They sedangkan Has digunakan untuk subjek He, She, dan It.

Positif

I/You/We/They + have + verb 3

He/She/It + has + verb 3

Negatif

I/You/We/They + have + not + verb 3

He/She/It + has + not + verb 3

Interogatif

Have + I/You/We/They + verb 3?

Has + He/She/It + verb 3?

Contoh:

1. (+) They have lived in Kuala Simapang for a long time

   (-) They have not lived in Kuala Simpang for a long time

   (?)Have they lived in Kuala Simpang for a long time?

2. (+) He has learned English for one  year

    (-) He has not learned English for one year

    (?) Has he learned English for one year?

    (-) I have not written a poem

    (?) Have I written a poem?

4. (+) He has done his homework

   (-) He has not done his homework

   (?) Has he done his homework?

5. (+) They have played football

   (-) They have not  played football

   (?) Have  they played football?

6. (+) Laura has gone to Semarang by train

   (-) Laura has not gone to Semarang by train

    (?) Has Laura gone to Semarang by train?

7. (+) We have watched the concert together

   (-) We have not watched the concert together

   (?) Have we watched the concert together?

8. (+) Ammar has learnt English for five years

   (-) Ammar has not learnt English for five years

   (?) Has Ammar learnt English for five years?

9. (+) Ameera and Ammar have bought a new car

   (-) Ameera and Ammar have not bought a new car

   (?) Have Ameera and Ammar bought a new car?

10. (+) The teacher has explained the lesson

     (-) The teacher has not explained the lesson

     (?) Has the teacher explained the lesson?

11. (+) My father has already seen that show

    (-) My father has not already seen that show

     (-) My brother and his friend have not gone to the cinema

Pendahuluan

Simple Past Tense digunakan untuk berbicara tentang aktivitas masa lalu. Simple Past sendiri biasanya menggunakan past verb/verb 2. Keterangan waktu yang biasa digunakan :

Last weekLast nightYesterdayetc.

-

Formula—Verbal

(+) S + Verb 2 + o/c

(-) S + did not + Verb 1 + o/c

(?) Did + S + verb 1 + o/c ?

—Nominal

(+) S + to be (was/were) + Adj/Adv/Noun

(-) S + to be (was/were) + not + Adj/Adv/Noun

(?) To be (was/were) + S + Adj/Adv/Noun

Catatan penting!Was⇒I, she, he, itWere⇒you, we, they, we

-

2. Select the “Report lost or stolen” option and fill in the required data, such as phone number, IMEI number, or serial number correctly.

40. how to use mobile phone


A mobile phone is a portable telephone that can make and receive calls over a radio frequency link while the user is moving within a telephone service area. The radio frequency link establishes a connection to the switching systems of a mobile phone operator, which provides access to the public switched telephone network (PSTN). Modern mobile telephone services use a cellular networkarchitecture, and, therefore, mobile telephones are often also called cellular telephones or cell phones. In addition totelephony, 2000s-era mobile phones support a variety of other services, such as text messaging, MMS, email, Internet access, short-range wireless communications (infrared, Bluetooth), business applications,video games, and digital photography. Mobile phones offering only those capabilities are known as feature phones; mobile phones which offer greatly advanced computing capabilities are referred to as smartphones.carssimol ne jadi begitu
